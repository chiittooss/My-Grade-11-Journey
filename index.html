Academic Artifact

Trying to adjust for the subjects I find difficult.

+
success chemistry earth science

Achievement

Throughout my academic journey, I experienced both achievements and struggles. Ever since I started school, I have consistently kept high marks and I have never dropped below an average of 90 and it's something I'm genuinely proud of!

It shows that even when lessons are getting progressively harder, expectations are increasing each year, I still manage to keep my academic performance up.

Struggle

My biggest academic struggle was adjusting to the new subjects, especially Earth Science and Chemistry. In Earth Science, I struggled to stay interested, especially when we started talking about rocks. I thought it would become one of my favorite subjects because I once considered becoming an astronaut. But even when we did come across topics outside Earth, I just couldn’t connect with the subject.

Chemistry was even more difficult for me. Topics like atoms, the periodic table, and computing masses were confusing and overwhelming. I had a very hard time understanding and remembering them. The lessons that especially gave me a headache were the ones that had to be caulcated with their own formulas. It didn't help when we had about 2 quarters with it..These subjects weren't something I could easily pick up, and that made it frustrating.

Although, these experiences helped me realize that not everything will come naturally to me. Sometimes I will have to work for it. I am still trying to improve these subjects by reviewing and focusing more on understanding instead of memorizing blindly.

Skill-Building Artifact

Coding, basketball, and art

+
coding basketball art

💻 Coding

I have been interested in coding for a long time because I used to watch people create games and websites. It inspired me to try making my own. The hard part was that I had no idea where to begin. I only had basic HTML knowledge and seeing so many different types of programming languages made me feel demotivated.

This school year, coding came back into my life in a more serious way. I still have a love-hate relationship with it because it can be VERY frustrating at times but I also feel proud whenever I solve errors. This is my first time really trying to code a game from start to finish and even if it is still simple, it already feels like a huge step for me. (I wanted to push myself and go out of my comfort zone since this will be my last project for G11)

I had to watch TONS of tutorials, test many code ideas and I still kept going even when not every video, every code worked. Small working features gave me motivation. Coding taught me patience, persistence and how to enjoy the process instead of only chasing for the finished result.

🏀 Basketball

I always wanted a sport of my own. After living a sedentary lifestyle because of COVID-19, I wanted to become more active. I asked my dad to enroll us in classes that involved running and because he is a basketball player, he immediately signed us up for basketball.

At first, I felt uncomfortable because it was a male-dominated sport. I was insecure and worried about being judged as a girl. I also felt nervous when male friends invited me to join games because I didn't think I was good enough.

But the more I played, the more I fell in love with it. I met kind people, and always asked for feedback. I corrected mistakes before they become habits. I am still learning but I can clearly see that I'm improving.

🎨 Art

Art has always been my passion. Ever since I was little, I loved drawing people. It is one of the main ways I express myself. Over the years I have made many artworks that show how much I have improved.

Over time, I started becoming more aware on what I should improve (proportions, shading, etc). Even small improvements in my work made a big difference in the overall look of my artwork.

What I love about art is that it shows progress in a very visual way. Unlike other subjects, I can clearly see the difference between my earlier work and my recent ones, which motivates me more to keep improving. Even though I still think I have a lot to learn, I’ve become more patient with the process. I now spend more time refining my work instead of rushing to finish it, which has helped me improve both my skill and attention to detail.

What Was Hard

One of my biggest challenges in school was managing deadlines, assessments, workload, and the pressure of handling many activities at the same time. Chemistry, again, was one of the most difficult subjects for me. Topics like molarity and the periodic table were confusing and stressful.

One of my biggest challenges in school has been managing deadlines, assessments, workload, and the pressure of handling multiple activities at the same time.

Again, chemistry has been one of the hardest subjects for me. Topics like molarity, the periodic table, and other concepts were confusing and difficult for me to fully understand. I often found myself struggling to keep up during discussions, which made it more stressful when assessments were near.

There were also a LOT of moments where different projects and activities would pile up all at once and it was very overwhelming and mentally draining for me. It was difficult trying to balance everything, especially when multiple subjects had different deadlines. I often felt like I had to rush tasks just to finish them on time which added more stress.

Aside from the workload, I also struggled with the pressure of maintaining my academic performance. When I saw my grade on the first quarter, I was a bit worried. It felt like I hadn't tried my best even if I did. I’ve always tried to keep my high grades so there’s a constant expectation for me to do well even when I’m already feeling tired or overwhelmed.

But I am learning how to deal with them better. I’m trying to improve my time management and study habits so I can handle pressure more effectively instead of avoiding it or getting stressed. These experiences are helping me understand that challenges are part of the learning process.

Small Progress That Mattered

Over time, I’ve become more aware of my physical and mental well-being. One of the main things that helped me improve was becoming more active in physical activities, especially sports like basketball. Through this, I’ve learned to move better, stay active, and be more aware of my body. It also helped me build discipline through consistent practice, even when I didn’t feel like it.

Another thing I’ve noticed is that my self-confidence has improved compared to before. I feel more comfortable trying new things and stepping out of my comfort zone, whether in school, outside, or personal projects. I don’t hesitate as much as I used to and I’m more willing to learn through mistakes instead of avoiding them.

I’ve also improved in terms of finishing tasks. Before, I would often struggle with starting or completing projects. Now I’m more capable of pushing myself to actually finish what I start. This is something I’ve noticed especially in schoolwork and personal projects like coding.

Overall, I think my wellness has improved not in one big change, but through small progress in different areas like physical activity, confidence, and discipline. I’m still improving, but I can see that I’m becoming more consistent and in control of myself.

Looking Ahead

I already started researching my future, especially the courses I might take in college. Right now, I'm mainly deciding between mechatronics or going into the medical field. Both interest me in different ways, which makes choosing even harder.

Journey Map

Main Plot.

🪜 The Climb

The start of Grade 11 honestly felt like starting over.

A lot of the people I was already familiar with had moved to different schools, so everything suddenly felt unfamiliar. New people, new environment and a completely different atmosphere. It felt weird having to adjust to all of it at once.. especially on the first day. At the same time, I was also entering STEM, which made things feel even more unfamiliar. It wasn’t just a new environment socially, but academically too. Everything felt new all at once, and I had to get used to it.

🌧 The Storms

As time went on, things started getting more overwhelming. It wasn’t just about adjusting to new people or new subjects anymore. It was the pressure of trying to keep up with everything at once. Deadlines started piling up. More workload kept adding. It was hard to manage everything without feeling stressed. There were a lot of times where multiple activities from different subjects had almost the same deadline and trying to keep up with all of them felt mentally exhausting. It got overwhelming fast, especially when I was already struggling to adjust to subjects like Chemistry and ES while also trying to maintain my usual academic performance. A lot of the pressure came from trying to balance EVERYTHING at the same time.

🔁 The Pivots

Over time, I started realizing that adjusting didn’t mean instantly being good at everything. It was more about learning how to deal with things little by little. Instead of expecting myself to understand everything right away, I started trying to be more patient with myself and with the process. I became more aware that not everything will make sense immediately and that improving takes time. I also started finding progress outside of academics too! Especially in coding, art, sports and even in how I manage myself. Those were the things that reminded me that progress doesn’t always have to be big to matter. Sometimes it’s just being a little more disciplined, a little more patient, or a little less afraid of making mistakes.

🏁 The Summit

I’m still figuring a lot of things out but I think I understand myself more now than I did before. Grade 11 pushed me into a lot of things, it forced me to adjust, be more disciplined and figure things out as I go. I’m still not completely sure where I’ll end up but I know I’ve changed a lot from where I started.
