Artifacts

Academic Artifact

Throughout my academic journey, I have experienced both achievements and struggles.

Success: Maintaining my 90+ grade streak

Ever since I started school, I have consistently maintained my high marks. I have never dropped below the average of 90 and it’s something I’m genuinely proud of.

It shows that even when lessons are getting progressively harder, expectations are increasing each year, I still manage to keep my academic performance up.

Struggle: Adjusting to the new subjects

Despite my consistent academic performance, my biggest struggle was adjusting to the new subjects..particularly the subjects 'ES' and 'CHEM'.

Those subjects gave me a really hard time. It was hard for me to remember the discussions in those subjects.

More info!

In ES, I struggled to stay interested, especially when we started talking about rocks. I thought it would be my new favorite subject, since I considered becoming an astronaut. But even when we did come across topics outside Earth, I just couldn’t connect with the subject and because of that it was harder for me to focus and actually remember what was being discussed.

Chemistry was even more challenging for me. Topics like atoms and the periodic table were confusing for me. I had a very hard time understanding and remembering them. One particular topic was about periodic elements where we had to compute its overall mass. The ones that gave me a headache were the lessons that needed to be solved and had their formulas on their own. It always made my head spin and it didn't help when we had about 2 quarters with it.

These subjects weren't something I could easily pick up, and that made it frustrating.

Although, these experiences made me realize that not everything will come naturally to me, there will always be times where I have to work for it. I’m still working on improving by reviewing more and trying to understand the lessons better instead of just memorizing them blindly.

Coding

I’ve actually been interested in coding for a long time. I used to watch videos of people creating games and websites, and it really inspired me to try making my own. It looked fun and creative, and I liked the idea of building something from scratch.

The problem was… I didn’t know how to start or WHERE to start. I only had basic knowledge of HTML from previous grades, and when I tried researching more, I realized there were so many different coding languages. It made me demotivated knowing..

I started getting more into coding when it was introduced again in school. This school year! That’s when I actually began trying to learn it more seriously. I still have a love-hate relationship with it. It gets frustrating when things don’t work out since I had to carefully check every code I did to figure out the error.

This is actually my very first time in trying to code a game! From start to finish and I'm really committed to finishing it. It’s still a 'simple', but the process itself is a vert a big step for me! I had to figure things out on my own, I had to watch a lot of youtube tutorials and test different types of codes. It was hard when not all codes have worked for me. There were times where I felt stuck and wanted to stop, but seeing small parts of the game actually work motivated me to keep going. Even simple things, like getting a feature to function properly, felt like a big achievement.

I still have a lot to learn, but this experience made coding feel more real to me. It’s not just something I watch anymore..I’m actually doing it with my own hands. Coding has taught me to be more patient and persistent and I’m starting to enjoy the process, not just the final result.

Basketball

I've always wanted to have my 'own' sport from the start. Ever since COVID-19 started, I've been living with a sedentary lifestyle and I really wanted to change that

Last year, I asked my dad to enroll us in summer classes that involved running, since I wanted a sport that involved a lot of leg action. As he is a basketball player, obviously he wanted us to try his sport. One day later, he immediately enrolled us in basketball classes.

At first, I felt uneasy and uncomfortable. This was a male-dominated sport. I've always felt like the guys there were judging me, since I was a girl. I've heard them talk badly about the other girls in the class.

Girls were considered 'weak' in the sport.

I was also insecure when male friends wanted to play basketball with me and invite me to basketball plays. I didn't feel like my skills were 'good enough' to join. But the more I played the sport, the more I fell in love with it. From the feeling of insecurity to the feeling of striving to become better. I've met so many nice people in basketball. With them, I was more motivated in improving my current skills I always asked for feedback on what I'm doing incorrectly to prevent it from becoming a habit and adjust the way I play. I became more aware of these mistakes and started correcting them.

It’s still a work in progress, I am still learning, but I can see that I’m improving.

Art

Art has always been my passion. Ever since I was little, I loved to draw people. It is something I’ve genuinely loved ever since I started.

It’s one of the ways I express myself. I’ve done different artworks throughout my years, and looking back at them, I can clearly see how much I’ve improved. Over time, I started becoming more aware on what I should improve (proportions, shading, etc). Even small improvements in my work made a big difference in the overall look of my artwork.

What I love about art is that it shows progress in a very visual way. Unlike other subjects, I can clearly see the difference between my earlier work and my recent ones, which motivates me more to keep improving. Even though I still think I have a lot to learn, I’ve become more patient with the process. I now spend more time refining my work instead of rushing to finish it, which has helped me improve both my skill and attention to detail.

Overall, art is something I’ve always enjoyed. It has helped me develop creativity, patience, and the ability to see improvement through consistent effort.

Challenge Artifact

One of my biggest challenges in school has been managing deadlines, assessments, workload, and the pressure of handling multiple activities at the same time.

Again, chemistry has been one of the hardest subjects for me. Topics like molarity, the periodic table, and other concepts were confusing and difficult for me to fully understand. I often found myself struggling to keep up during discussions, which made it more stressful when assessments were near.

There were also a LOT of moments where different projects and activities would pile up all at once and it was very overwhelming and mentally draining for me. It was difficult trying to balance everything, especially when multiple subjects had different deadlines. I often felt like I had to rush tasks just to finish them on time which added more stress.

Aside from the workload, I also struggled with the pressure of maintaining my academic performance. When I saw my grade on the first quarter, I was a bit worried. It felt like I hadn't tried my best even if I did. I’ve always tried to keep my high grades so there’s a constant expectation for me to do well even when I’m already feeling tired or overwhelmed.

But I am learning how to deal with them better. I’m trying to improve my time management and study habits so I can handle pressure more effectively instead of avoiding it or getting stressed. These experiences are helping me understand that challenges are part of the learning process.

The Climb

At the start of Grade 11, I felt unsure if I could handle the increased workload and expectations. I struggled with inconsistent study habits and often relied on last-minute effort instead of planning ahead. My goal was to become more organized, improve my performance, and feel more confident in my abilities

The Storms

One of the hardest moments I faced was when I felt overwhelmed by multiple deadlines and didn’t know where to start. I felt stressed, unmotivated, and frustrated with myself for falling behind. Instead of continuing that cycle, I realized I needed to change how I approached my work and take small steps to regain control.

The Pivots

I began managing my time more effectively by using a planner and breaking tasks into smaller, more manageable steps. I also started asking for help when I didn’t understand something, which helped me learn more efficiently and reduced my stress.

The Summit

Now, I am more consistent with my work and no longer depend on last-minute studying. I feel more confident in asking questions and taking initiative in my learning. I have also developed better time management skills, allowing me to stay organized and balanced.
